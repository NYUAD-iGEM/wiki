LAMP Box (PCR tubes + Smartphone based fluoroscence measurement + electronic heating)

Device Setup

Equipment Required

Off-the-shelf PCR Tubes are used inside a 3-D printed enclosure. An Arduino Uno R3 with HM11 Bluetooth low energy (BLE) module (Seeed Technology Co., Ltd) is used to operate the heater and the LED. Power is supplied to the Arduino using a 5V USB Connection. The isothermal heater is made up of Two 10 W, 3.3 Ω ceramic wire wound resistors (Digi-key; Part # 3.3W-10-ND). Arduino’s digital pin provided the heater with 5 V input voltage through a N channel MOSFET (MOSFET N-CH 60 V 55 A TO-220; Digi-key; Part # 497-6742-5-ND). A temperature sensor (Digi-key; Part # AD22100STZ-ND) is also used to control the temperature. The LED is an RGB LED (3 W RGB tricolor; Digi-key; Part # 516-2246-1ND) fitted with a triple-bandpass filter (Edmund Optics #87-237) (which costs ~300 USD). (Note here that they use a triple bandpass because they test using 3 different fluorophores, FAM, SYTO 9, and CYK-5).

Finally, the fluoroscence is measured using a smartphone (camera +Bluetooth) + another optical filter (determined by the fluorophore used but costing ~ 150 USD) mounted on the 3-D printed box. A custom app uses the camera to take and analyze the fluoroscence readings and uses the Bluetooth to control the Arduino.

Key Findings

  • Use of easily available ceramic resistors as a heater
  • Use of Arduino + Bluetooth for wireless control
  • Use of smartphone +LED + Expensive optical filters for fluoroscence detection
  • Use of easily available PCR tubes for chemical reactions
  • Cons: No lysis procedure, so not sure about the feasibility of that
