Smart Cup (Microfluidic Chip + Smartphone based fluoroscence measurement + Chemical heating)

Device Setup

Equipment Required

A modified thermos cup as the reaction platform, which contains a tray to hold the Mg-Fe alloy heating pouch, easily available in market and used with MRE meals. The cup should also store water which comes in contact with the pouch to start the heating process. A PCM (Phase Change Material) layer is used to provide constant temperature as it starts changing phase (melting) at a constant temperature (here 65 °C) for a long time (~2 hrs) even when the pouch is exceeding that temperature. The generated gases during heating are vented out through holes in the lid. The heat from PCM is transferred through a aluminium heat sink to the microfluidic chip. The chip accepts a lysed solution which is filtered through a membrane to preserve nucleic acid and wash out other components. Then LAMP reagents are added to the chip to start the amplification. The excitation is done by the led flash in the smartphone and the fluoroscence generated is recorded by the smartphone camera by taking an image every minute for an hour. Optical filters are also used in the process, but it is unclear in the paper where. The data generated in the phone is processed using MATLAB in a computer to get the results (an app can be made to do this on a phone).

Key Findings

  • Use of Mg-Fe pouch + PCM + Thermos Flask as a non-electric heater
  • Use of silica membrane in microfluidic chip for NA purification
  • Use of smartphone for fluoroscence detection (for further investigation, see LAMP Box (PCR tubes + Smartphone based fluoroscence measurement + electronic heating) )
  • Cons: Uses already lysed solution which might not be available, Handling of microfluidic chip without a micro-pipette might be an issue
