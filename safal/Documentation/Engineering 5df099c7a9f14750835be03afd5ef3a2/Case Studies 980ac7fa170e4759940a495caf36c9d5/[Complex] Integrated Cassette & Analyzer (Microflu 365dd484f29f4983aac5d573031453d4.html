[Complex] Integrated Cassette & Analyzer (Microfluidic Chip + LFA Detection + Electrical Heating)

Equipment Used + Workflow:

  • Very Custom Made Microfluidic Chip with Compressible Pouches and Valves for storing and propagating liquids by the use of plungers (see fig. 2)
  • Analyzer: Actuators (Mini DC Motors) + Plungers, Small Vibrating Motor, Heaters, Micro Vacuum Pump, Microcontroller
  • The sample is placed in the mixing chamber where lysis buffer is propagated by the moving down the plunger (Once a plunger is moved down, it stays in the position permanently to avoid any backflow). Then the Vibrating motor is used to mix the buffer and sample
  • By closing appropriate valves and using the vacuum pump, the mix is made to flow through a silica membrane embedded inside the chip to collect the nucleic acid, and the rest goes to waste pad.
  • Subsequently, wash buffers are passed through the membrane using plungers and then the chip is heated and vacuumed to evaporate any ethanol.
  • Then, an elution buffer is passed through the membrane to elute the nucleic acids, which are then transferred to the PCR chamber.
  • By closing valves to seal the chamber and using the heaters to first melt the paraffin seal to release the reagents, that are rehydrated by the buffer and then perform thermocycling, PCR is done.
  • Finally, the PCR product is mixed with a lateral flow buffer and 10% of it is pushed to the loading pad of the lateral flow strip. Then other lateral flow reagents are also pushed to the strip and the acids are detected.

Key Findings

  • Fully automated NAAT system without much manual handling
  • Use of Silica Membrane for extraction of Nucleic Acid
  • Use of plunger driven propagation, valves, and vacuum pump propagation in microfluidic chip
  • Use of paraffin film to store dry reagents
  • Cons: Chip does not seem easily multiplexable, The analyzer seems to be power intensive (no information provided in the papers), The fabrication process is overall complex
